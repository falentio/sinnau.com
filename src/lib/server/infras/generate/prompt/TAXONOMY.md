# Bloom's Taxonomy for Learning Content Classification

Bloom's Taxonomy is a framework for classifying learning objectives by cognitive complexity. It helps identify what kind of thinking a learning item requires from the learner, from simple recall to advanced evaluation or creation.

Use this taxonomy to classify learning content such as quiz questions, flashcards, prompts, exercises, or study tasks by the highest cognitive skill needed to complete them successfully.

## Levels

| Level | Classification | Description                                                                                         | Common Question Cues                                       |
| ----- | -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1     | Remember       | Recall facts, definitions, terms, or basic concepts.                                                | define, list, identify, name, state, recall                |
| 2     | Understand     | Explain meaning, summarize ideas, or interpret information.                                         | explain, describe, summarize, classify, compare, interpret |
| 3     | Apply          | Use knowledge, rules, methods, or procedures in a concrete situation.                               | apply, calculate, solve, use, demonstrate, implement       |
| 4     | Analyze        | Break information into parts, identify patterns, causes, relationships, or assumptions.             | analyze, differentiate, compare, contrast, infer, organize |
| 5     | Evaluate       | Judge value, correctness, quality, or effectiveness using criteria.                                 | evaluate, justify, critique, defend, assess, decide        |
| 6     | Create         | Produce something new by combining ideas, designing a solution, or generating an original response. | create, design, propose, construct, develop, formulate     |

## Classification Guidelines

Classify a learning item based on the thinking process required, not only the wording or format of the item.

- If the learner only needs to recognize or recall information, classify it as `Remember`.
- If the learner must show meaning or explain a concept in their own words, classify it as `Understand`.
- If the learner must use a known rule, formula, or procedure, classify it as `Apply`.
- If the learner must inspect structure, relationships, causes, or differences, classify it as `Analyze`.
- If the learner must make a judgment using evidence or criteria, classify it as `Evaluate`.
- If the learner must generate a new solution, design, plan, or original output, classify it as `Create`.

## Examples

### Remember

Item: What is the capital city of France?

Classification: `Remember`

Reason: The learner only needs to recall a fact.

### Understand

Item: Explain why photosynthesis is important for plants.

Classification: `Understand`

Reason: The learner must explain the meaning or purpose of a concept.

### Apply

Item: Use the formula `area = length × width` to calculate the area of a rectangle with a length of 8 cm and a width of 5 cm.

Classification: `Apply`

Reason: The learner must use a known formula in a specific case.

### Analyze

Item: Compare mitosis and meiosis and identify two key differences.

Classification: `Analyze`

Reason: The learner must break down two processes and identify relationships or differences.

### Evaluate

Item: Which of these solutions is the most effective for reducing plastic waste? Justify your answer.

Classification: `Evaluate`

Reason: The learner must judge options using reasoning or criteria.

### Create

Item: Design a simple experiment to test how sunlight affects plant growth.

Classification: `Create`

Reason: The learner must produce an original plan or solution.

## Notes for Content Generation

- Quizzes and flashcards can belong to any Bloom level, depending on the reasoning required.
- A flashcard that asks for a definition is usually `Remember`, while a flashcard that asks the learner to explain, compare, or justify may be a higher level.
- A quiz question with complex wording is not automatically high-level; it may still require only recall.
- A learning item should be classified by the highest required cognitive operation.
- Prefer clear labels: `Remember`, `Understand`, `Apply`, `Analyze`, `Evaluate`, and `Create`.
