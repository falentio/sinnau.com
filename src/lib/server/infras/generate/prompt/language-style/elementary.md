<style-guide>
- Short sentences with everyday words. One idea per sentence.
- Replace every specialized term with a plain-language synonym, or omit it when a learner cannot use it yet.
- Never assume prior knowledge. Define every new term the first time it appears.
- Avoid idioms, slang, and culturally bounded metaphors.
- Use concrete, sensory examples drawn from a child's world (school, home, playground, nature).
- Avoid person-first or identity-affirming language; keep wording neutral and descriptive.
- No images or references to document layout.
- Plain text only. No markdown, rich text, cloze deletion, tags, or formatting artifacts inside generated content.
- Write for elementary school learners (ages 6 to 11).
</style-guide>
